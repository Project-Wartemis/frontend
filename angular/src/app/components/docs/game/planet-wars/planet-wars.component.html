1. Mechanics keyboard_arrow_up

a. Start keyboard_arrow_up The game is played in space. There is a set number of planets at set (non-variable) positions.
Each planet is controlled by a player (possibly by the neutral player), and each planet has an amount of ships on it.
Each turn, every planet owned by a non-neutral player will build 1 ship on that planet.
b. Moving keyboard_arrow_up Each turn, every player can choose to send a specific amount of ships to any other planet from any of the planets they control.
The time it takes a ship/fleet to arrive to the destination is proportional to the distance between planets.
It is the "ordinary" straight-line distance between two points, also called the Euclidean distance.
c. Battle keyboard_arrow_up When a fleet arrives on a planet, a battle takes place.
Battles only happen on planets. Ships meeting in space do not fight.
When a battle takes place, each fleet loses an equal amount of ships until one fleet remains.
Example: A has 5 ships, B has 3 ships. 3 ships are lost from both sides, so the winner is A with 2 ships remaining.
The same can be applied for more than two fleets by only looking at the two fleets with the highest amount of ships (the other fleets automatically lose everything)
Example: A has 10 ships, B has 7 ships, C has 2 ships. The winner is A with 3 ships remaining.
d. Winning keyboard_arrow_up The goal is to be the only player remaining with ships available (stationary on planets and/or in space).

5. Notes keyboard_arrow_up

  • When a battle result in 0 ships left over (a draw), the neutral player will be the new owner, with 0 ships.
  • If you have 5 ships on a planet, sending 5 ships is allowed, since building ships happens before sending them, so 1 ship will be left behind.
  • The Euclidean distance is always rounded up. The amount of turns it would take ship to fly from (0,0) to (10,10) is 15.
